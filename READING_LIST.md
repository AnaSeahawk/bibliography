# Reading List — Sovereign Biophysics Archive

Books sourced to deepen and cite the archive. Organized by theme.
Files live in their respective topic folders; unsourced titles are listed here as acquisition targets.

## alchemy/

**Primary historical sources and scholarly scholarship — citations for Women of Alchemy and translation map.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *A Suggestive Inquiry into the Hermetic Mystery* | Mary Anne Atwood (1850) | Directly referenced in archive. First serious argument that alchemy was a coded language for self-transformation. |
| *Psychology and Alchemy* | C.G. Jung | Foundation for reading alchemical symbolism psychologically. *Nigredo / albedo / rubedo* sequence; *coniunctio*; the sealed vessel. |
| *Mysterium Coniunctionis* | C.G. Jung | Late Jung; deepest treatment of the sacred marriage (*hierosgamos*), solve/coagula, and the union of opposites. |
| *Alchemy: An Introduction to the Symbolism and the Psychology* | Marie-Louise von Franz | Clearest entry point into Jungian alchemy. Commentary on the *Aurora Consurgens*. Von Franz was closest to Jung on this material. |
| *Anatomy of the Psyche: Alchemical Symbolism in Psychotherapy* | Edward Edinger | Maps each alchemical operation (calcination, dissolution, coagulation, etc.) to psychological process. Practical for grounding the method writing. |
| *The Secrets of Alchemy* | Lawrence Principe | Best modern scholarly history. Distinguishes what practitioners actually did vs. symbolic interpretation. |
| *The Jewish Alchemists* | Raphael Patai | Includes Mary the Jewess chapter; Alexandrian lineage; Jewish alchemical transmission. |
| *A Dictionary of Alchemical Imagery* | Lyndy Abraham | Reference volume. Every symbol, vessel, operation indexed with primary source citations. |
| *Chrysopoeia* (ouroboros diagram) | Cleopatra the Alchemist | The ouroboros diagram with *Hen to Pan*. **Acquired:** `alchemy/Cleopatra-Chrysopoeia-ouroboros.png` — 1280×1409 px greyscale. Primary source image. |

## water/

**The science grounding the electrochemical measurements — EZ water, plasma-activated water, structured biology.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *The Fourth Phase of Water* | Gerald H. Pollack | Directly referenced. EZ water, H₃O₂ lattice, exclusion zone, biological battery. Essential citation for every measurement. |
| *Cells, Gels and the Engines of Life* | Gerald H. Pollack | Earlier, more technical. Argues that cell function depends on gel-state water, not ion pumps. Foundational for the biological argument. |
| *The Rainbow and the Worm: The Physics of Organisms* | Mae-Wan Ho | Quantum coherence in living systems; liquid crystalline water as information medium. Bridges physics and biology beautifully. |
| *Living Rainbow H2O* | Mae-Wan Ho | Specifically on structured water in life. More accessible than Rainbow and the Worm; directly relevant to fraction behavior. |
| *Water and Salt: The Essence of Life* | Barbara Hendel & Peter Ferreira | Crystallography of water and mineral structure. Useful for the Saturn retentate / salt section. |

## mythology/

**Primary source material for the Inanna essay and the mythological architecture of the archive.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *Inanna: Queen of Heaven and Earth* | Diane Wolkstein & Samuel Noah Kramer | THE primary source. Actual transliteration of Sumerian tablets with Wolkstein's narrative retelling. Any direct quote from Inanna myth should cite Kramer's translation here. |
| *Inanna: Lady of Largest Heart* | Betty De Shong Meador | Translations of Enheduanna's hymns — the world's first named author (c. 2300 BCE), a woman, writing to Inanna. Directly supports the lineage argument. |
| *The Moon and the Virgin* | Nor Hall | Depth-psychological exploration of lunar feminine, the vessel, containment, and cyclical transformation. |
| *Descent to the Goddess* | Sylvia Brinton Perera | Jungian reading of Inanna's descent as initiatory template for feminine psychology. Closest scholarly parallel to the archive's Inanna interpretation. |

## women-healers/

**Historical record of women's medical, alchemical, and midwifery knowledge — primary sources and history of suppression.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *The Trotula: A Medieval Compendium of Women's Medicine* | Edited/translated by Monica H. Green | Critical edition of the Trotula texts (Salerno, 11th-12th c.). Primary source for Trotula of Salerno. Green's introduction is the best scholarship on women's medicine in this period. |
| *Witches, Midwives, and Nurses: A History of Women Healers* | Barbara Ehrenreich & Deirdre English | Short, sharp, well-sourced account of how institutional medicine displaced women healers. Direct historical support for the suppression argument. |
| *The Woman in the Body: A Cultural Analysis of Reproduction* | Emily Martin | Medical anthropology. How women's bodies are described in modern medicine — passive, mechanical, absent of agency. Counterpart to the archive's reclamation work. |
| *I Secreti della Signora Isabella Cortese* (facsimile) | Isabella Cortese (1561, Venice) | Directly referenced. First book published by a female alchemist. National Library collections. |
| *Experimenti* | Caterina Sforza | 400+ recipes. Archival, not easily available in English — but the scholarly work on it is. |
| *A Suggestive Inquiry* | Mary Anne Atwood | (also listed under alchemy) |

## urine-therapy/

**Primary literature on amaroli, shivambu, and urine therapy — foundational practice references.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *The Water of Life: A Treatise on Urine Therapy* | John W. Armstrong (1944) | The foundational Western text. First systematic modern account. Any amaroli archive needs this as its historical English-language anchor. |
| *The Golden Fountain: The Complete Guide to Urine Therapy* | Coen van der Kroon | Most comprehensive survey of the practice, historical lineage, and protocols. Western-accessible. |
| *Manav Mootra* | Raojibhai Manibhai Patel | Classic Indian text. *Shivambu* in the Vedic context. Supported by Gandhi (noted in the text). Direct lineage to amaroli protocols. |
| *Shivambu Kalpa Vidhi* (from Damar Tantra) | Source text | The original Vedic scripture on the practice; 107 verses attributed to Shiva. Any serious archive wants this citation. |

## ayurveda/

**Dietary framework, *anupana*, and the metabolic logic underlying the distillation baseline.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *Charaka Samhita* (4 vols) | Translated by P.V. Sharma | The foundational Ayurvedic text. Source for *ojas*, *tejas*, *prana*, dietary classifications, and the logic of *anupana* (carrier substances). |
| *Sushruta Samhita* | Translated by K.M. Bhishagratna | Surgical/anatomical counterpart to Charaka. Relevant to understanding body-as-laboratory and substance taxonomies. |
| *Ayurveda and the Mind* | David Frawley | Most readable modern bridge. Covers *ojas/tejas/prana* in practical language — good for grounding the manifesto terminology. |
| *The Yoga of Herbs* | David Frawley & Vasant Lad | Botanical-Ayurvedic language for *anupana* relationships — relevant to the botanical preparation sections. |

## grief-and-body/

**Deepening the grief essay and the somatic/psychological dimension of the work.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *The Wild Edge of Sorrow* | Francis Weller | The single best contemporary book on grief as ecological and ancestral, not individual pathology. Direct companion to the grief essay. |
| *Women Who Run With the Wolves* | Clarissa Pinkola Estés | Mythological psychology of the feminine. *La Llorona*, the Wild Woman, descent and return. Directly supports the lineage argument and grief framing. |
| *The Pregnant Virgin: A Process of Psychological Transformation* | Marion Woodman | Jungian feminine psychology; the body as bearer of the unconscious; the vessel that contains and transforms. |
| *Bone: Dying Into Life* | Marion Woodman | Woodman's cancer journals. The body as teacher; literal and symbolic dissolution; the alchemical process lived. |
| *The Courage to Grieve* | Judy Tatelbaum | Practical but thorough. Useful counterpart showing how grief was systematically stripped of its natural process in the 20th century. |

## feminist-science/

**Epistemological grounding — supporting the "knowledge lived in bodies before it was written" claim.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *A Feeling for the Organism: The Life and Work of Barbara McClintock* | Evelyn Fox Keller | Biography of the geneticist who talked to her corn. Embodies the archive's epistemology exactly: direct attentive relationship with the subject as legitimate knowledge. |
| *The Science Question in Feminism* | Sandra Harding | Philosophical analysis of how science constructs its knowing subject — and who gets excluded. Background scholarship. |
| *Sexing the Body: Gender Politics and the Construction of Sexuality* | Anne Fausto-Sterling | Medical construction of sex and anatomy — directly supports the clitoris/O'Connell section. |
| *Simians, Cyborgs, and Women* | Donna Haraway | Situated knowledge; the observer is part of the experiment. Grounding for the single-subject methodology defence. |

## alexandrian-science/

**Historical context for the Alexandrian alchemists — what they actually knew.**

| Title | Author | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *The Forgotten Revolution: How Science Was Born in 300 BC and Why It Had to Be Reborn* | Lucio Russo | Argues the Greeks achieved scientific method in the Hellenistic period; it was suppressed, not lost. Context for what the Alexandrian women alchemists had access to. |
| *Hellenistic Science and Culture in the Last Three Centuries B.C.* | George Sarton | Comprehensive overview. Anatomy, chemistry, mathematics at Alexandria. |
| *The Beginnings of Western Science* | David C. Lindberg | Standard textbook; covers Herophilus, Erasistratus, Alexandrian anatomy section directly. |
