## Extracted quotes

### Soma as the Agni-Soma pair — solar and lunar (Foreword)

> "Soma is part of the eternal pair of Agni-Soma. Agni is the fire element; Soma is the water element, moonlike. This pair is the Vedic equivalent of the Taoist yin-yang principle, balancing of the female and the male, energizer (female) and the energized (male)."

**Source:** Swami Veda Bharati, Foreword

---

### Moon, mind, and the waters (Foreword)

> "This moon is the same that is 'in the waters'... This mind of the waters and the moon blows towards us eternal peace."

**Source:** Swami Veda Bharati, Foreword, citing Rigveda I.105.1 and IX.11.3

### The essence of bliss (Author's Preface)

> "The key to physical, psychological and spiritual well-being lies in our Soma, which can perhaps best be defined as the essence of bliss or Ananda arising from the core of our being. This is not an outer Soma only, but an 'inner Soma' or 'nectar of immortality' in our own deeper awareness."

**Location:** Author's Preface, Part I

---

### Yoga as cultivation of Soma (Part III)

> "Classical Yoga can be described as the cultivation of Soma as the inner nectar of bliss, ananda and immortality. It is not a mere outer practice or exercise but rests upon a deeper inspiration and aspiration to the highest."

**Location:** Part 6 (Part III — Soma Yoga)

---

### Yoga as the alchemy of Soma (Part III)

> "Yoga is the 'alchemy of Soma', the fruit of which is abiding in our own immortal essence. Without an inner flow of Soma, there is no real Yoga, one could say."

**Location:** Part 6

---

### Soma in the crown — the descent of nectar (Part III)

> "The crown chakra, yogically known as the 'thousand-petal lotus', holds the main energy of Soma in the subtle body. In Tantric Yoga, the crown chakra is associated with Soma or Chandra, the Moon, and the mind... The inner process of Yoga consists of the ascent of the Kundalini Fire from the root chakra and the corresponding descent of the Soma nectar from the crown chakra."

**Location:** Part 6

---

### Prana and Rasa — fluid and essence (Part IV, Prana Rasayana)

> "Rejuvenation or rasayana works through rasa, which means both 'fluid' and 'essence'. Prana itself is a kind of rasa or essence of the body."

**Location:** Part 7, section "Prana and Rasa"

---

### The breath behind the breath (Part IV)

> "You are not the breath, which is but an instrument. You are that which breathes, which is the energy and awareness behind the breath. This inner Prana of the spirit is what allows the outer physical breath to occur."

**Location:** Part 7

- **Presentation translation table**: Agni-Soma = solar-lunar = the alchemical solar/lunar separation. The Vedic pair maps directly onto the distillation process described in the presentation.
- **Crown chakra / Soma nectar descending**: the Tantra physiology of the practice — soma as endogenous secretion from above, meeting agni from below. This is the energetic anatomy the practice works with.
- **Rasa = fluid and essence**: this double meaning underpins the entire triple vocabulary in the translation table. Rasa is simultaneously the taste, the juice, the plasma, and the refined essence.
- **Moon in the waters**: Vedic frame for the living solution as lunar in quality — reflective, receptive, cyclic.
